# v0.8 Social and Sharing

## Goal

Let users share useful recipes and cooking wins without turning GlucoPlate into a noisy social network.

## Status

Foundation in progress. The main community delivery remains planned after the active v0.6 personalization work and the core v0.7 meal-planning slice.

## User outcomes

- Users can share recipes through native share sheets and durable links.
- Users can export readable recipe cards.
- Households can share grocery lists or meal plans with explicit permissions.
- Users can cook together in authenticated live rooms.
- Authors can publish selected AI-generated recipes as transparent community stories.
- Readers can save, discuss, share, and report community recipes safely.

The read-only community detail can be built as an isolated foundation before every dependency is complete. Publishing and engagement must not launch publicly until authentication, moderation, and production-data requirements are satisfied.

## Testing

### Sharing and privacy

- [ ] Shared links open the intended resource.
- [ ] Private recipes, prompts, profiles, locations, and shopping history remain private.
- [ ] Native share fallback works on unsupported browsers.
- [ ] Authorization tests cover author, household member, visitor, moderator, and enterprise boundaries.

### Community

- [ ] Public detail covers published, missing, unpublished, under-review, and removed states.
- [ ] Publication creates an immutable versioned snapshot.
- [ ] AI provenance, nutrition uncertainty, and safety state are always visible.
- [ ] Comments and stories are sanitized.
- [ ] Reactions and repeated mutations are idempotent.
- [ ] Reports and moderation decisions are auditable.
- [ ] Mobile, keyboard, screen-reader, offline, and 200% zoom flows pass.

### Live rooms

- [ ] Host and participant flows pass with two or more authenticated sessions.
- [ ] Presence, step synchronization, chat, help, leave, expiration, and reconnect behavior pass.
- [ ] Enterprise and invite-code boundaries prevent cross-tenant access.
- [ ] Retention and abuse limits are enforced.
